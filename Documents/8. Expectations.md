## 1. Personal Conduct Expectations
- Take responsibility 
- Listen before giving solutions
- Respect othersâ€™ time
- Do not interrupt others
- Communicate early when blocked
- Ask questions instead of guessing
- Write clear messages
- Be reliable
## 2. Technical Quality Expectations
- Code must be readable and consistent in project, team
- PRs must be small and focused
- Tests must accompany new features
- No skipping error handling
- Problem space should be exhausted
- Bugs should not be negotiated, instead should be accepted and fixed
- Respect any sign of error over your own hypothesis about the system
- Prioritize team practices over self practices
## 3. Delivery Expectations
- Never work on parallel tasks
- Always prefer finishing current task over starting a new one
- Make sure app is deployed in beta and stage
- Check logs and make sure there is no errors after deploy
- Monitor behavior changes
## 4. Process Expectations
- Attend standups on time
- Keep documentation updated
- Respect branching strategy
- Cross Team Communication should go through email either from PO or Team-lead
## 5. Communication Expectations
- Prefer async communication when possible
- Share decisions publicly, not privately
- Use structured messages
- Be concise
- Always include context
- Always negotiate over an existing entity (Code of Conduct, Diagram, etc.)
- Only Talk about proven facts
- Do not socialize in team workspace
- Do not repeat previous conversations
## 6. Ownership Expectations
- You maintain the health of what you own
- You respond to incidents related to your component
- You improve the system proactively
- You ensure performance, reliability, and scalability
- You should prevent bugs in the earliest stage
- You should not rely on others to test your system
- You should accept and check reported bugs rather than giving quick responses
## 7. Cultural Expectations
- Admit mistakes quickly
- Be humble
- Be realistic
- Do not compare team members or company members
- Help others generously
- Give and receive constructive feedback
- Keep learning
- Be collaborative
- Perfect your work first, then guide others
- Let your product talk for you
